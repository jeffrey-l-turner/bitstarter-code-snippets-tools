Bitcoin is at a crossroads. Just a few major currency exchanges or "cambios" serve the majority of the Bitcoin economy. With such a significant majority of Bitcoin transactions operating through so few cambios, the entire Bitcoin ecosystem is at risk. From over-regulation or with just a few exchanges failing, the Bitcoin economy could collapse as a result. We propose the development of a peer-to-peer value exchange platform. This platform will scale Bitcoin by allowing parties to directly transact with one another in a private and secure fashion. The reliance on a select few cambios can be eliminated. Thus, a much more robust Bitcoin economy can develop and flourish.

FAQ

Why do we need such a platform?
Currently, Bitcoin does not have a virtual currency exchange mechanism. Parties must use individual currency and value exchanges such as Mt. Gox. Unfortunately, the existing exchange model is vulnerable to over-regulation or systemic failure. A "virtual" cambio must be developed. Cambix proposes to provide a system in which privacy of transactions can be maintained while insuring that attackers may not subvert online trading. A virtual exchange will not only help market liquidity for Bitcoin, it will also make the overall system more immune to individual exchange failure.
What are the market requirements for the platform?
The Bitcoin community currently lacks a ubiquitous mechanism by which parties can discover what other parties wish to sell along with verfying that the exchanges were properly executed. Virtual goods, including virtual financial instruments such as Bitcoin or altcoin exchanges are particularly challenged. Our goal is to provide a platform by which these exchanges can be made "in the cloud" and allow parties to buy or sell any asset class they define. Of course, reputation and escrow mechanisms may be required—particularly for the exchange of physical goods.
Exchanging physical goods and traditional financial instruments requires trust and non-anonymity amongst the parties. Will this platform be only useful for virtual goods and assets?
Of course, today, many people will feel much more comfortable exchanging bitcoins in a non-anomous environment where they can be assured of little counter-party risk . Trust and reputation networks have not sufficiently built-up in the virtual world to minimize counter-party risk. This platform will help reduce that risk in that parties can be assured of secure, private, and reliable transaction data is being exchanged. Virtual goods, services and financial instruments are, of course, more serviceable by an online exchange platform than physical one. However, as the Bitcoin ecosystem evolves, trust and reputation networks will emerge leveraging platforms like the one we're developing here.
How will this platform compare with LocalBitcoins or Bitcoin-OTC?
LocalBitcoins is primarily focused on enabling individuals to physically exchange Bitcoins for other physical assets (e.g. cash) and is centralized on a single website. It is not focused on providing an online trading mechanism or platform.
Bitcoin-OTC on the other hand, is designed as an online trading system. However, it lacks a spam-free machine-to-machine communications mechanism. In addition, our system is designed to support offline usage while keeping the virtualized exchange online performing automated trading. In essence, the market needs a platform which supports discoverability, privacy, integrity and operates on a 24x7 basis. (A detailed discussion of these requirements can be found here.)
What other components are needed to make the platform successful?
The platform requires a secure, discoverable mechanism in which parties can communicate with one another without others interferring with that communication (such as "spam" or false transactions). There are several other open source projects which can assist this one. Specifically, BitMessage ("PyBitmessage"), ensures that parties receive cryptographically secure messages over a broadcast network and has spam prevention features. In addition, Open-Transactions will allow for parties to transact business anonymously and verifiably through the network. By combining these facilities with a virtual exchange, users will be able to interact and transact business for virtual, financial and physical goods.
Isn't OpenCoin's Ripple currency (XRP) going to solve this problem?
First and foremost, OpenCoin's Ripple currency is not truly "open." It is a proprietary system designed to establish Ripples as the primary medium of exchange amongst the parties. (See: "Ripple Scam" .) Ripples are "pre-mined" property of OpenCoin. As such, the approach neither fits with the philosophy or "fairness" of Bitcoin. In addition, OpenCoin has different overall goals. Their system is broadly focused on identifying and exchanging value via a network of trust. Ultimately, OpenCoin wants to allow participants to exchange different types of assets across a "trust network" of intermediaries in way that all parties receive their desired value. Under their proposed framework, party A could buy gold from party C in exchange for dollars if party B was willing to sell Euros to party C and receive dollars in exchange. Their system could have very broad applications but is not focused on the immediate needs of the Bitcoin community. Hence, a more narrowly focused virtual exchange platform that fits within the spirit of Bitcoin must be developed. Cambix proposes a system in which Bitcoin, itself, can be used as that medium of exchange.
How will the contributions be used? May I specify how the project should be prioritized with my sponsorship?
The $100,000 in contributions are expected to pay for approximately 2 full time equivalent engineering resources for 6 months along with ancillary testing and infrastructure resources (two Mac or Chromebook computers as well as an Amazon Web Services account). The donations will be used to develop the software with a concentation on building the HTML5 user interface components first. Specifically, we intend to focus on creating a virtualized infrastructure with a single online transaction instance back-end initally. We will then integrate the online transaction platform to tie the trading activity together with mutliple exchange instances. This will allow us to focus on human factors in our development cycle, while allowing the other open source components we require to mature.
And, yes—we will be allowing donation activity to drive the feature set priorities.
Will I receive any special benefits by being an early contributor or contributing a minimum amount?
We have not yet decided how to provide additional incentives. Currently, we think that one benefit commercial operators could provide would be to enable a more reliable and low latency network infrastructure for the communications betwen the virtual exchange instances. This would provide advantages to traders using the platform with priority because their trades could be executed more quickly. We're exploring this approach. Please check back with us as our plan evolves.
Will this project be like a new Bitcoin or altcoin wallet?
No. This project is designed to enable a new trading platform that can support a wide variety of financial instruments but does not compete with the wallet implementations themselves. Our intention is to enable a broad range of new virtual digital assets—including enabling exchange into "altcoins" such as litecoin .
Is this project only serving Bitcoin? What about other Alt-currencies?
Of course, any virtual or physical instrument could be traded using the platform. Other altcoins and currencies could be supported as end-users might choose to make them part of their asset to be bought or sold. Initially, our focus will be to serve Bitcoin community. Bitcoin BTC will act as both the medium of exchange and price signalling mechanism.

Please donate via the button above. You can also follow us on GitHub and work collaboratively with us there. We welcome your feedback. You can send thoughts, questions or suggestions to: info at cambix.org.
